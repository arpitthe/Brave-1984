The World State: A "Perfect" Society

Set in AF 632 (2540 AD), the World State has eliminated suffering through:

  • Genetic Engineering: Humans are bred in Hatcheries as Alphas (leaders), Betas (technicians), or lower castes (Gammas, Deltas, Epsilons).
  • Conditioning: Children are brainwashed to love their social role and consume goods.
  • Soma: A drug that induces instant happiness, replacing religion and art.
Theme: Technology dehumanizes society in exchange for stability.

Bernard Marx: The Misfit Alpha

Bernard, a psychologist, feels alienated due to his small stature (rumored to be a cloning error). Despite his high caste, he:

  • Criticizes the State’s promiscuity and soma use.
  • Secretly desires monogamy and genuine emotion.
  • Uses his status to visit a "Savage Reservation" (New Mexico), where uncivilized humans live.

Lenina Crowne: The Conformist Beta

Lenina is a vaccination worker conditioned to enjoy casual sex and consumerism. Her conflict arises when:

  • She develops unorthodox feelings for Bernard.
  • She visits the Savage Reservation and is horrified by its poverty and religion.

John the Savage: The Outsider

Born to a World State citizen (Linda) but raised on the Reservation, John embodies humanity’s lost virtues:

  • He reads Shakespeare, valuing love, suffering, and individuality.
  • He returns to London with Bernard, becoming a celebrity for his "exotic" views.
  • He rejects soma, promiscuity, and the State’s empty pleasures.
Theme: True humanity requires freedom, even if it brings pain.

The Downfall: John vs. the World State

John’s rebellion escalates:

  • He publicly destroys soma rations, inciting a riot.
  • He retreats to an isolated lighthouse, self-flagellating to purify himself.
  • Mobsters film his suffering as entertainment, driving him to suicide.
Ending: The State’s control is absolute; individuality is crushed.
